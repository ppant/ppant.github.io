While doing routine cleaning of my personal library I was surprised to see the Perl book collection I have made over the period of time. My Perl dev journey started in a full-fledged manner way back in fall 2007. Prior to that was mainly developing using C, C++, assembly language. My first impression with Perl was not very exciting mainly due to ugly syntax and the way the OO is achieved and being from C++ background initially it was really difficult to grasp. But over the years working with language and while developing a large scale web application I learned a lot of nitty-gritty of the language and still learning...  Today I can vouch for Perl for its speed, portability, great module system [CPAN](https://metacpan.org/) and excellent dedicated community. Thanks to all the module authors and contributors on [Perl Monks](https://www.perlmonks.org/) and [StackOverflow](https://stackoverflow.com/questions/tagged/perl). You guys are amazing!   
Now, the books which helped me immensely to wrote better Perl programs.
